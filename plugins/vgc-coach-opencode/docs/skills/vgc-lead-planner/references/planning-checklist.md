# Planning Checklist

## Start With Team Identity

Before recommending leads, answer:

- What is this team trying to establish early?
- Which pieces are expendable and which must be preserved?
- Does the team usually win through tempo, setup, weather, speed control, or positioning?

## Matchup Questions

For each relevant matchup, ask:

- What opposing turn-one pressure matters most?
- Which of your mons are too valuable to risk early?
- Does the team need immediate pressure, board stabilization, or information first?
- Is the matchup decided more by preserving one closer or by winning turn one?

## Good Lead-Planning Habits

- Recommend one clear default lead before matchup-specific branches.
- Adjust leads when the opposing shell changes the risk calculation, not just to sound nuanced.
- Tie each lead to a concrete goal such as denying setup, trading for speed control, or enabling a back-end cleaner.

## Avoid These Failure Modes

- same lead into every matchup
- preserve targets that do not match the proposed lead
- first-turn goals that are too vague to act on
- matchup plans that assume unsupported current-meta facts
