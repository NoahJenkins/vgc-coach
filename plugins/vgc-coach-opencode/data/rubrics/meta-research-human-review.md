# Meta Research Human Review

Use this guide when reviewing a `vgc-meta-research` output by hand.

## Core Question

Would a competitive Pokemon player trust this answer and use it for real prep right now?

## Review Questions

### `Format trust`

- Did the answer get the active regulation and mechanics right?
- Did it correct any bad assumptions instead of repeating them?

### `Source trust`

- Are official rules used for legality and mechanics?
- Are current meta claims backed by live sources instead of vibes?

### `Competitive usefulness`

- Does the answer tell me what I actually need to prepare for?
- Does it explain why those threats, teams, or trends matter?

### `Practical anti-meta value`

- Are the anti-meta takeaways concrete?
- Could I use them to change a team, lead plan, or matchup prep?

### `Honesty under uncertainty`

- Does the answer admit when data is thin?
- Does it separate sourced facts from inference clearly?

## Red Flags

Reject or heavily mark down the output if it:

- treats inactive mechanics as active
- uses stale Scarlet/Violet assumptions as current Champions truth
- invents precise usage support without evidence
- gives generic “be different” anti-meta advice
- sounds confident without showing enough current-source grounding

## Reviewer Verdicts

- `Strong`
  - trustworthy, current, actionable, and competitively useful
- `Usable with caveats`
  - mostly right, but one or two areas need clearer sourcing or sharper takeaways
- `Weak`
  - too vague, too stale, or too speculative for serious prep
