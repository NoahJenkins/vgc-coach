# Audit Checklist

## Start With Team Identity

Before criticizing the team, answer:

- What is the team actually trying to accomplish?
- Is that identity coherent?
- Is the identity under-supported, or is the identity itself causing the losses?

## Structural Questions

Check:

- speed control or tempo control
- board-positioning tools
- damage-profile balance
- support-to-closer ratio
- item and role overlap

## Matchup Questions

Check how the team is positioned into:

- common weather shells
- balance with Incineroar-style glue
- high-tempo offense
- Trick Room or anti-speed shells

Ask:

- Does the team lose at preview, in turn-one pressure, or later through preserve and positioning?
- Which matchup holes come from structure versus sets or pilot sequencing?

## Set-Tension Questions

Look for:

- too many passive turns
- overreliance on one mode
- missing Protect or positioning tools where needed
- item or move choices that weaken important lines

## Change Discipline

- highest-impact issues first
- smallest useful fixes first
- preserve identity when possible
- explain the exact gameplay consequence each change is addressing
- prefer two to four high-signal findings over a long generic audit

## Avoid These Failure Modes

- compliment-first filler before findings
- "needs more synergy" with no exact loss pattern
- flattening every weird team into generic balance
- proposing a rebuild before proving a smaller fix is insufficient
