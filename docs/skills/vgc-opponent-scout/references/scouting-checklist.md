# Scouting Checklist

## What Is Actually Known

Before scouting, separate:

- public results or player history
- public team lists
- archetype-level format tendencies
- pure inference with no hard evidence

## Good Scout Questions

- What shells are most likely from the public evidence?
- Which one or two techs would materially change prep if present?
- What preview, lead, or preserve risks follow from those likely shells?
- What prep adjustments are useful even if the exact closed team differs?

## Confidence Discipline

- high confidence for directly supported public information
- medium confidence for repeated tendencies across multiple public artifacts
- low confidence for archetype extrapolation or thin evidence

## Avoid These Failure Modes

- pretending public clues reveal an exact six
- listing every possible tech instead of the relevant few
- failing to explain why a read is low confidence
- giving prep notes that do not connect to any likely shell
